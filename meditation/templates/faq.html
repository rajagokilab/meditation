FAQs

1. How do I know if I need therapy?

+

If you're feeling overwhelmed, anxious, low, or stuck—or simply want to better understand yourself—therapy can help. You don't need a crisis to benefit from mental health support.

2. What is mental wellness?

+

Mental wellness is a state of well-being in which an individual realizes their own abilities, can cope with the normal stresses of life, can work productively and fruitfully, and is able to make a contribution to their community.

3. What types of therapy do you offer?

+

We offer a variety of therapy types, including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), trauma-informed therapy, and person-centered counseling. We can discuss which approach is best suited for you during our initial consultation.

4. What can I expect in a therapy session?

+

A typical therapy session lasts 50 minutes. It's a confidential and safe space for you to talk about your thoughts and feelings without judgment. The therapist's role is to listen, provide guidance, and help you develop coping strategies and a deeper understanding of yourself.
